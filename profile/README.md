# Open Engineering Channels

The communication channels for the Open Engineering ecosystem.

Open Engineering Channels provides the public-facing experiences that make the Open Engineering ecosystem discoverable, understandable, and accessible.

While the Open Engineering Platform provides reusable engineering capabilities, Open Engineering Channels provides the places where people and systems interact with them.

It is the home for websites, documentation, developer portals, presentations, blogs, demonstrations, and other communication channels that connect the ecosystem with the outside world.

⸻

## Purpose

Engineering is only valuable when its knowledge can be shared.

Open Engineering Channels exists to publish, explain, demonstrate, and promote the ideas, capabilities, and projects that make up the Open Engineering ecosystem.

Rather than treating every website or documentation portal as an isolated project, Open Engineering Channels provides a common home for all public-facing communication.

⸻

## What is a Channel?

A Channel is a public interface through which the Open Engineering ecosystem communicates with people or other systems.

Channels may target:

* engineers
* architects
* contributors
* educators
* students
* organizations
* AI assistants
* software systems

Each channel presents the same engineering knowledge in a form appropriate for its audience.

* logos

* icons
* illustrations

⸻

## Design Principles

Every channel should be:

* open by default
* easy to discover
* easy to navigate
* accessible
* reusable
* consistent with the Open Engineering visual identity
* maintainable
* composable

⸻

## Vision

Open Engineering Channels aims to create a coherent communication ecosystem where every website, document, presentation, demonstration, and learning experience feels like part of a single, open engineering platform.

Whether someone discovers Open Engineering through a website, a conference presentation, a QR code, a video, or technical documentation, they should encounter the same architecture, language, and visual identity—presented through the channel best suited to their needs.
